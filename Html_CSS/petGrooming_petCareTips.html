PawPup Tips for Dogs

Your dog is your priority. Taking care of your dog makes you build a healthy relationship and ignites your soul!

Here are some tips to make sure that your puppy gets all the care and attention that they so lovingly deserve.

Bathing tips:-

  1. Make sure that you bathe your dog every one to three weeks.
  2. Use a mild shampoo to avoid irritation
  3. Start by first brushing the dog and keep adding luke warm water so that your dog does not catch cold.
  4. Do not spray the shampoo or conditioner close to eyes, ears or nose
  5. Make sure that you check ears and clean it well
  6. If your dog has itchy or flaky skin, use a moisturising shampoo and avoid chemicals.
  7. Use flea or tick shampoo to get rid of the ticks.
  8. Gently massage the tail mane and make sure that you rinse it well.
Extra Tips: The right shampoo for your dog

Nail Trimming Tips:-

  1. Use a quality clipper and make sure that you do not put the whole nail in a clipper
  2. Use small size clippers for better control
  3. Hold the dog's paw firmly and cut small amount of nails at a time
  4. Make sure that you chip the nail until you see a while with the black dot
  5. Check and cut your dog's nail regularly
  6. Trim the nails in a well-lit room
  7. Use your fingers to gently seperate the toes of the dog for clipping
  8. Do not apply much pressure on the toes
Extra Tips: The right clippers and step by step nail clipping procedure

Teeth Brushing Tips:-

  1. Set a routine to brush your dog's teeth to avoid building plaque
  2. Gently massage the gums and brush the teeth
  3. Use a toothbrush that is made for dogs and make sure that the bristles are softer and rightly angled
  4. Avoid using flavored chemically treated toothpastes
  5. Use very light pressure and finger along her upper gums and teeth
  6. Make sure that you brush gently to avoid inflammation or bleeding along the jaw
  7. If your pet is not willing, try another time
  8. Set up a dental cleaning session if you are not sure of how to go about it.
Extra Tips: Be teeth ready!
